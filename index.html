Yuan-Yen Peng

ATLAS Higgs Physics, ML Reconstruction, and Analysis Infrastructure

HWW physics with missing information

I work in experimental high-energy physics at National Tsing Hua University, contributing to ATLAS Higgs analyses, detector-software infrastructure, and reproducible computing environments. My current physics focus is $H \to WW^* \to \ell\nu\ell\nu$, where two unobserved neutrinos hide the rest-frame information needed for quantum-entanglement, spin-correlation, and W-polarization observables. I develop physics-constrained ML and analysis workflows to recover missing information in a form that can be validated against detector effects, systematic variations, and collaboration review.

Four connected contributions

HWW quantum entanglement and W polarization

Work on quantum information in HEP, including W polarization, spin-correlation observables, and missing-information reconstruction in $HWW$.

ML reconstruction for missing information

Physics-constrained models for recovering rest-frame information hidden by two unobserved neutrinos, with validation and reproducibility in mind.

Run 3 HWW coupling analysis

Participation in ggF and VBF electroweak coupling analysis using Run 3 data, with student co-mentoring and FastFrames workflow support.

HGTD and reproducible computing

HGTD production-database work, workstation maintenance, containerized environments, local CernVM-FS caching, and group analysis support.
