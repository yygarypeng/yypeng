Yuan-Yen Peng

Perceiving the beauty of the universe, analyzing the principles of nature.

I am an experimental particle physicist working in ATLAS on quantum-entanglement and Higgs-coupling analyses in \(H \to WW^* \to \ell\nu\ell\nu\). My research focuses on ML-assisted reconstruction of under-constrained events, W-boson polarization, and analysis-level validation. I also co-develop the HGTD production database for the ATLAS Phase-II upgrade.

I am seeking PhD opportunities centered on a well-defined measurement, search, or detector challenge, where advanced computational methods can improve scientific sensitivity, precision, or performance. Here is my CV.

At a Glance

Physics

Current work on HWW quantum-entanglement and Run 3 Higgs-coupling analyses, with broader interests in Higgs, electroweak, diboson, and BSM-sensitive measurements.

Reconstruction

Developed attention-based physics-constrained networks for reconstructing W boson (neutrinos) kinematics in partially observed events, alongside studies of invertible architectures and adaptive multi-loss training.

Validation

Analysis-level validation spanning distributions, bias, resolution, truth-to-reconstruction behavior, SR/CR regions, and central-framework integration.

Computing

Run 3 analysis, HGTD database development, and reproducible ROOT and Python workflows using Docker/Podman containers with CernVM-FS integration.
