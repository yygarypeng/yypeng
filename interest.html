Interest

From HWW reconstruction to measurable quantum information

My current work starts from $H \to WW^* \to \ell\nu\ell\nu$, where spin correlations, quantum-entanglement observables, and W-polarization information are encoded in a final state with two unobserved neutrinos. I have worked on W-rest-frame reconstruction and ML-based recovery of missing information for this topology, with the goal of turning an under-constrained final state into observables that can survive detector effects, systematic checks, and ATLAS review. These directions grow out of my MSc thesis, ATLAS note work, Run 3 HWW analysis participation, and software contributions for ATLAS-facing workflows.

Precision measurements in search-inspired phase spaces

My HWW work has trained me to think about missing information, background control, and validation against physics observables. I want to extend that experience to precision Standard Model measurements in search-inspired phase spaces, including $WW$, $WZ$, polarization-sensitive diboson channels, and differential observables that improve both SM interpretation and future BSM sensitivity.

ML reconstruction for missing information

My recent work uses attention-based, invertible, and physics-constrained models to recover missing rest-frame information. The next step is not only better prediction, but better evidence: models validated against physics observables, stress-tested under systematic variations, compared to conventional reconstruction, and exported into reproducible workflows that can be inspected by an analysis team.

New-physics searches with missing information

The same reconstruction problem appears in compressed spectra, SUSY-inspired scenarios, dark-matter-motivated signatures, feebly interacting particles, and other missing-energy final states. I would like to test how methods developed in a controlled HWW measurement can transfer to searches where ambiguity, limited statistics, and background modeling are central challenges.

Deployable ML and real-time analysis

I also want to push reconstruction methods closer to deployment: ONNX or similar export paths, low-latency inference, trigger-aware reconstruction, online monitoring, and real-time analysis constraints. This direction connects offline ML ideas to Run 3 and HL-LHC environments where physics performance, reliability, and maintainability must be optimized together.

Analysis infrastructure and collaboration tools

Across these interests, I value software that makes physics work easier to review and reproduce. My experience includes ROOT and FastFrames workflows, Python analysis tooling, containerized environments, CernVM-FS access, HGTD database work, local computing support, and collaboration-facing interfaces. I want to contribute not only to physics results, but also to the infrastructure that lets a group do careful analyses faster.
