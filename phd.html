Direction

I am seeking a physics-strong ATLAS PhD centered on a concrete measurement or search, with advanced methods used where they improve reconstruction, event discrimination, inference, or detector-aware analysis.

What I bring

Physics foundation

HWW quantum-information and coupling analyses, W-polarization, spin correlations, and Run 3 validation.

Method foundation

ML-assisted reconstruction for missing information, with emphasis on physics constraints and analysis-level robustness.

Collaboration readiness

ATLAS frameworks, detector service, reproducible environments, and handover-ready analysis workflows.

What I am looking for

I am interested in PhD projects where I can contribute to a concrete ATLAS measurement or search while developing physics-informed ML for reconstruction, event discrimination, and inference. Building on my work in HWW, quantum observables, and under-constrained final states, I aim to improve precision, sensitivity, or phase-space reach across Higgs, electroweak, diboson, and other relevant Run 3 analyses.
