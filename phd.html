PhD

I am looking for an ATLAS PhD group where I can develop as a physicist with strong reconstruction, inference, and computational skills.

My preferred direction is physics-first and method-strong: Higgs/EW, diboson, or WZ analysis at the core, with ML, statistical inference, reproducible workflows, or detector knowledge used where they improve reconstruction, interpretation, or measurement robustness.

Research Fit

Higgs and electroweak physics

HWW reconstruction, W-polarization interpretation, quantum-correlation observables, and Run 3 validation.

Diboson and WZ-sensitive reconstruction

Neutrino-rich or ambiguous final states where reconstruction and inference affect polarization, coupling, or electroweak sensitivity.

Trigger-aware or detector-facing ML

Workflows where fast decisions, limited information, or upgrade constraints shape the physics reach.

Inference and reproducible workflows

Analysis tools, validation pipelines, and computational methods that make ATLAS measurements more robust.

Detector and service work

HGTD production-database experience and interest in long-term ATLAS operations and service work.

Immediate Contribution Areas

HWW / diboson / EW validation

SR/CR checks, object-level validation, shape comparisons, bias and resolution studies, and systematic cross-checks.

Missing-information reconstruction

ML-assisted reconstruction for neutrino-rich or under-constrained final states, validated through physics observables and baseline comparisons.

Deployable ATLAS workflows

ROOT, FastFrames, Python, ONNX, Docker/Podman, CernVM-FS, and handover-friendly analysis environments.

Detector-facing service

HGTD production-database experience and interest in upgrade or service tasks connected to ATLAS physics output.

Directions

Higgs, electroweak, and diboson measurements

Higgs, diboson, W/Z polarization, SMEFT, quantum-information, and Run 3 coupling studies as routes toward stronger ATLAS measurement ownership.

Physics-guided machine learning methodology

Attention-based, invertible, and physics-constrained models for missing-information reconstruction, kept interpretable and tied to measurable analysis gain.

Detector, trigger, and object reconstruction

HGTD service work and HWW object-level validation motivate reconstruction- or detector-facing projects where performance studies affect physics reach.

Reproducible analysis infrastructure

FastFrames, ROOT, ONNX, containerized workflows, CernVM-FS, Git, and group-computing support for workflows that survive handover and reuse.
