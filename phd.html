Research

I am seeking a PhD in experimental particle physics centered on a well-defined measurement, search, or detector challenge. I am particularly interested in advanced methods that provide demonstrable improvements in reconstruction, event discrimination, statistical inference, or detector understanding.

What I bring

Physics

HWW quantum-entanglement and coupling analyses, W-polarization, spin correlations, and Run 3 validation.

Methods

ML-assisted reconstruction for missing information, with emphasis on physics constraints and analysis-level robustness.

Collaboration

ATLAS frameworks, detector service, reproducible environments, and documented, maintainable analysis workflows.

What I am looking for

Building on my work in HWW physics, spin-sensitive observables, and under-constrained reconstruction, I aim to contribute to Higgs, electroweak, diboson, BSM, or detector-related research. I am open to different experiments and methodologies when they support a compelling physics objective and lead to a concrete result.
