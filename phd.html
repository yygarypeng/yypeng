PhD

For prospective PhD supervisors

I am applying for ATLAS PhD projects where physics analysis, reconstruction, statistical interpretation, and collaboration-ready software meet. Machine learning is a tool in my work, not the center of my identity.

My current base is $H \to WW^* \to \ell\nu\ell\nu$: two neutrinos, hidden kinematics, W polarization, quantum-information observables, Run 3 control regions, and deployable analysis workflows.

Research fit map

Higgs, electroweak, and diboson measurements

Why: Precision measurements stress-test the Standard Model and probe new physics indirectly.

Fit: HWW, W polarization, quantum-information observables, and Run 3 control regions.

Grow: Own more of the measurement chain, from selections and validation to interpretation.

BSM and exotic signatures

Why: Searches often depend on subtle event features or partially observed information.

Fit: Two-neutrino HWW trained me on under-constrained final states and hidden kinematics.

Grow: Apply reconstruction and classification to search sensitivity, not just standard analyses.

Detector, trigger, and object reconstruction

Why: Physics reach starts with detector performance, trigger choices, and object definitions.

Fit: HGTD database qualification plus HWW dependence on leptons, missing energy, and event reconstruction.

Grow: Contribute where detector/reconstruction validation directly shapes physics output.

Statistical modelling and interpretation

Why: Results depend on uncertainty treatment and separating physics from analysis artifacts.

Fit: I validate shapes, truth-to-reco correlations, ambiguity, bias, resolution, and baselines.

Grow: Build stronger inference and uncertainty-aware validation skills for final results.

Reproducible ATLAS analysis infrastructure

Why: ATLAS analyses must survive review, preservation, handover, and student onboarding.

Fit: FastFrames, ROOT, Python, ONNX, Docker/Podman, CernVM-FS, Git, and group computing support.

Grow: Build portable, reviewable workflows useful beyond my own analysis task.

Physics-guided ML for reconstruction

Why: Some key event-level quantities are not directly observed but still drive sensitivity.

Fit: Attention-based, invertible, and physics-constrained models for missing-information reconstruction.

Grow: Keep ML interpretable, deployable, and tied to measurable physics gain.

Immediate fit

  • Physics: HWW, Higgs/BSM, diboson/EW, W polarization, quantum-information observables, and missing-neutrino final states.
  • Technical base: FastFrames, ROOT, Python, ONNX, Docker/Podman, CernVM-FS, HGTD database work, and group computing support.
  • Goal: ATLAS measurement ownership, with reconstruction, statistics, and ML used only when they improve physics sensitivity or robustness.
