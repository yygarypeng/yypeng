Projects

HWW quantum information measurement

Physics question: Can quantum entanglement between the two W bosons in \(H \to WW^* \to \ell\nu\ell\nu\) be measured at the LHC?

My contribution: From the kickoff stage, I connected missing-information reconstruction with spin-correlation observables and studies of different W-boson polarization states.

Evidence: My MSc thesis and contributions to the ATLAS analysis document cover W-polarization and reconstruction studies, as well as analysis-level validation.

Why it matters: This work provides a transferable foundation for physics-informed reconstruction and inference in Higgs, diboson, and other partially observed collider signatures.

Missing-information reconstruction in HWW

Physics question: How can latent neutrino and boson kinematics be inferred when two unobserved neutrinos prevent direct reconstruction of spin-sensitive rest-frame variables?

My contribution: I developed attention-based, physics-constrained reconstruction with adaptive multi-loss training, studied invertible architectures, and integrated the reconstruction workflow into the central analysis framework.

Evidence: Validation covers reconstructed distributions, bias, resolution, truth-to-reconstruction behavior, and robustness within the central analysis framework.

Why it matters: The same inference problem appears in other collider signatures where invisible particles or detector limitations leave the event only partially observed.

Run 3, HGTD development, and reproducible workflows

Physics question: How can analysis validation, detector-upgrade infrastructure, and reproducible computing support reliable ATLAS physics through review and long-term use?

My contribution: I contributed to Run 3 HWW (ggF+VBF) first-look studies, SR/CR validation, framework development, and production-database components for the ATLAS HGTD upgrade.

Evidence: The work uses FastFrames, ROOT, Docker/Podman, CernVM-FS, Git, Django, and Vue in reviewable, reusable, and multi-user workflows.

Why it matters: It demonstrates experience connecting physics validation and detector contributions with documented, maintainable software in a large collaboration.
