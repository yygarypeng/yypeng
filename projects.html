Projects

HWW quantum information measurement

Problem: $H \to WW^* \to \ell\nu\ell\nu$ contains spin-correlation and W-polarization information, but the two neutrinos make key rest-frame variables under-constrained.

My role: I contributed from the kickoff stage, focusing on missing-information reconstruction and its connection to W-polarization and spin-correlation interpretation.

Output: MSc thesis and ATLAS internal-note contribution on HWW quantum information, W polarization, and ML-assisted reconstruction.

Relevance: This work prepares me for Higgs/EW precision, diboson polarization, and quantum-information measurements where reconstruction methods must remain interpretable and analysis-validatable.

Missing-information reconstruction

Problem: Two unobserved neutrinos prevent direct reconstruction of rest-frame variables needed for polarization and spin-correlation observables.

My role: I developed and validated ML-assisted reconstruction strategies for W and neutrino kinematics, emphasizing physics checks rather than standalone ML scores.

Output: An attention-based, physics-constrained reconstruction model integrated into the central QE analysis framework.

Relevance: The key tests are shape, bias, resolution, truth-to-reco behavior, robustness, and comparison with non-ML baselines.

Run 3, HGTD, and reproducible workflows

Problem: ATLAS analyses require reliable validation, detector-facing service, and computing environments that survive review, handover, and reuse.

My role: I contributed to Run 3 HWW first-look and control-region studies, supported MSc student onboarding, co-developed HGTD database components, and maintained local ATLAS/ML workflows.

Methods: FastFrames, ROOT, Python, Docker/Podman, CernVM-FS, Git, Django/Vue, and group-level workflow support.

Relevance: These tasks show readiness for reviewable ATLAS work combining analysis validation, detector service, and reproducible software.
