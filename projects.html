Projects

HWW quantum entanglement and W polarization

Problem: $H \to WW^* \to \ell\nu\ell\nu$ carries quantum-entanglement, spin-correlation, and W-polarization information, but two neutrinos hide key rest-frame variables.

Contribution: MSc thesis and ATLAS note work on W-rest-frame reconstruction for quantum entanglement and W-polarization interpretation.

Extension: A route toward collider quantum-information observables that remain meaningful after detector effects and systematic checks.

ML reconstruction for missing information

Problem: Two unobserved neutrinos make standard kinematic reconstruction under-constrained, while the analysis still needs rest-frame variables.

Contribution: Attention-based, invertible, and physics-constrained models with reproducible training, ONNX export, and validation outputs.

Extension: Reconstruction methods that can be compared with conventional approaches, stress-tested, and reused in related measurements or searches.

Run 3 HWW coupling analysis

Problem: Run 3 measurements need continuity, portable environments, and contributor onboarding.

Contribution: Participation in ggF/VBF electroweak coupling analysis, MSc student co-mentoring, and workflow tooling.

Extension: Practical experience connecting physics interpretation with reproducible group analysis workflows.

HGTD production database

Problem: Detector production needs reliable data management, quality-control views, and interfaces usable by multiple teams.

Contribution: Frontend and backend components for organizing, querying, and visualizing HGTD production data.

Extension: Collaboration-facing software that makes complex detector and production information easier to review.

Workstation and CernVM-FS

Problem: ATLAS analysis depends on reproducible software environments, reliable local computing, and access to collaboration software stacks.

Contribution: NTHU EHEP workstation maintenance, Docker/Podman workflows, high-performance AI resources, and local CernVM-FS caching.
